eli-neutral: Connecting Mobile Devices.
eli-neutral: With every person you know carrying a phone, a tablet, or both, IT needs reliable ways to connect these devices.
eli-neutral: These are tools that access company resources, store sensitive data, and link to our networks.
eli-neutral: Each device can communicate differently.
eli-neutral: In this session, we will cover wired and wireless connections and the standards behind them.
eli-neutral: You will learn how they work and why they matter for CompTIA A plus and real world troubleshooting.

eli-neutral: Wired connectivity.
eli-neutral: USB, the Universal Serial Bus, is the most common cable.
eli-neutral: Older devices used Mini USB or Micro USB; these still appear on legacy gear.
eli-neutral: Today most devices use USB C, which is reversible and supports fast charging and high speed data.
eli-neutral: USB C can also carry video protocols like DisplayPort, HDMI, and Thunderbolt through the same port.
eli-neutral: Apple’s older devices used the Lightning connector; many newer Apple devices now use USB C.

eli-neutral: Wireless connectivity.
eli-neutral: NFC, Near Field Communication, allows very short range data exchange for tap to pay and access control.
eli-neutral: Bluetooth creates a Personal Area Network for peripherals such as headsets, keyboards, and mice.
eli-neutral: Bluetooth can also tether a phone to share internet, typically slower but lower power than Wi Fi.
eli-neutral: Hotspot mode lets a phone act as a Wi Fi router for multiple devices; availability depends on device software and carrier plan.

eli-neutral: Recap.
eli-neutral: Wired: Mini or Micro USB on legacy gear, Lightning on some older Apple devices, and USB C as the modern standard for power, data, and video.
eli-neutral: Wireless: NFC for short range payments and authentication; Bluetooth for peripherals and optional tethering; hotspots for multi device sharing.
eli-neutral: Knowing these options helps on CompTIA A plus and in real world support.
